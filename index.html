About

Azure Developer Stories is a technical blogging contest that assists developers with the learning program. The initiative is designed to teach new technologies on MS Learn and write technical blogs, articles or video content with demos & code samples, hence enabling them to share their experiences while building on Azure because we believe stories have the potential to impact the mass, across the boundaries of nations and globes.

Participate

  • If you are an amateur, we recommend you
    • register here and attend regular meetups on effective writing and latest technologies that will help you upskill.
    • take the Azure Developer Challenge to learn and test your skills on Azure.
  • Write a technical blog on one of the categories using examples, scenarios using Microsoft Azure platform.
  • Publish your story on dev.to, Medium or any other blogging platform.
  • Submit your story with your, and your blog details on or before 7th April 2021.
  • The review panel will review the content and rate the submissions; the top 5 winners and other winners will be announced on 20th April 2021.
  • The next top 25 blog submissions will each receive swag pack [T-shirt + Stickers] before 30th April 2021.
  • Top 5 winners receive a Surface Pro X each.

What Makes Your Story Qualified?

  • The contest is open only for Indian nationals currently residing in India.
  • You must be the sole author of the story.
  • Your story should be written in English.
  • Your story should be on a topic that fits under one of the Azure Developer Stories categories (do not submit more than one story per category).
  • Your story can be a written article, a blog, or a video recording that has been published and publicly available via the internet.
  • Your story has to be original, published between 1st January 2021 to 7th April 2021 (both dates inclusive). Plagiarized, reconstruction, or republished stories will not be accepted.
  • Your story must contain in-depth technical content including examples, technical details, & working source code. Your story must contain relevant scenarios and use cases on the Microsoft Azure platform.
  • Best stories also focus on knowledge sharing and best practices.
  • You give Konfhub & Microsoft permission to publish and share the content on the Microsoft online resources, newsletters, and social media (you will still own all the rights to your submission).
  • You must not be a Microsoft Employee.
